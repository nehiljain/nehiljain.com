Arithmetic Intensity & the Roofline

GPU perf 101

Two limits on every GPU: compute and memory bandwidth. Your workload design picks which one hurts.

arithmetic intensity = FLOPs bytes moved
1

Arithmetic Intensity = how much math a kernel does per byte moved. High AI → math heavy. Low AI → memory heavy.

2

The slanted line is the bandwidth roof = your HBM. This is a hardware constraint. Below it, you're memory bound.

3

The flat line is the compute roof = peak FLOPs of the GPU's cores. Once you hit it, you're compute bound.

4

Where they meet is the ridge point. Max bytes transfer and max FLOPs. Workloads to its left waste FLOPs waiting on memory; to its right, they actually use the silicon.

5

Decode (one token at a time, KV-cache reads) lives on the left. Tiny AI ≈ 1. The GPU is mostly… waiting.

6

Prefill (1024+ tokens at once) lives on the right. Lots of reuse → AI explodes → you finally see those big TFLOP numbers.

7

So what? To speed up decode: raise AI. This is what inference engineering is nowadays.

TL;DR → the roofline tells you what to optimize!
